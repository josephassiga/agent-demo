# The Song of Ice and Fire

In Westeros where dragons soar and winter looms,
A tale of power plays out in ancient rooms,
From the Stark walls of Winterfell so cold,
To King's Landing where the throne's desire unfolds.

Seven kingdoms clash in ruthless, bloody strife,
Where honor often costs you everything—your life,
The Lannisters scheme with gold and cunning ways,
While Daenerys breaks chains through fiery maze.

Jon Snow knows nothing, yet he learns to lead,
As White Walkers march with an unholy need,
The dragons rise from eggs long thought to be stone,
And a khaleesi claims her rightful throne.

Stark children scattered to the winds of fate,
Some seek revenge while others choose to wait,
From bastards born to queens of distant lands,
Each character holds power in their hands.

Politics and passion twist the plot around,
Where loyalty is rare and death is found,
With swords that sing and prophecies so deep,
A kingdom falls asleep, and ice walkers creep.

In Westeros, the game continues still,
Where might makes right upon each battle's hill,
And when the dust settles from the final war,
What lies beyond remains to explore evermore.
